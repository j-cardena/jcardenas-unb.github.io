Building the Smart Grid for Atlantic Canada

I develop machine learning and optimization methods that help utilities integrate renewable energy, manage distributed resources, and operate the grid more efficiently.

Associate Professor Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering University of New Brunswick, Fredericton NB Power Industrial Research Chair Smart Grid Technologies

Research Focus

Renewable Integration

Dynamic thermal rating, hosting capacity analysis, and forecasting methods to maximize renewable penetration on existing infrastructure.

Distributed Energy Resources

Control and optimization of DER aggregations—solar inverters, EVs, and thermostatically controlled loads—for grid services.

Load Forecasting

Deep learning and probabilistic methods for short-term load and generation forecasting at multiple scales.

Virtual Power Plants

Hierarchical optimization and control architectures for coordinating distributed assets as dispatchable resources.

Smart Grid Communications

Routing protocols and network optimization for Advanced Metering Infrastructure and utility IoT deployments.

Demand-Side Management

Algorithms for peak shaving, voltage regulation, and ancillary services using flexible customer loads.

Current Projects

Real-Time Dynamic Thermal Rating & DER-Analytics Platform

Hiring

Developing a production-ready Dynamic Thermal Rating system in partnership with NB Power. The platform combines IEEE-738 thermal models with ML-based weather nowcasting to unlock 15–30% additional transmission capacity, supporting NB's target of 1,600 MW new renewables by 2035.
